What is ICT?

Information and communications technology (ICT) is an extensional term for information technology (IT) that stresses the role of unified communications and the integration of telecommunications (telephone lines and wireless signals) and computers, as well as necessary enterprise software, middleware, storage and audiovisual, that enable users to access, store, transmit, understand and manipulate information.

ICT SPECIALIZED

ICT stands for Information and Communication Technology. It refers to the convergence of information technology (IT) and communication technology to create a seamless platform for managing, processing, storing, and disseminating information.

Things you will learn about ICT



Skills Development

1. Programming skills: ICT students learn programming languages like Java, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript which are essential for website development.

2. Problem-solving skills: ICT students develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, which are valuable in many areas of life.

3. Collaboration skills: ICT projects often involve teamwork, helping students develop collaboration and communication skills.

Creativity and Innovation

1. Design and development : ICT students can express their creativity through designing and developing software, apps, and websites.

2. Innovation and experimentation: ICT encourages innovation and experimentation, allowing students to explore new ideas and technologies.

3. Gamification and simulation: ICT can be used to create engaging and interactive experiences, such as games and simulations.
